# ICE-GOV-001 — Project ICE Charter

**Status:** APPROVED  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** 2026-07-14  
**Owner:** HANA-X AI

## Working title

**HANA-X Copilot, The First Commercially Available SAP S/4HANA Copilot**

## Mission

Create the definitive technical publication introducing HANA-X Copilot as an enterprise-grade Agentic AI copilot for SAP S/4HANA. The publication will define **Intent-Centric Enterprise Computing (ICE)** and demonstrate how natural-language intent can be translated into secure, governed enterprise execution through released SAP S/4HANA interfaces while preserving Clean Core principles.

## Strategic thesis

Enterprise computing is moving from application-centric interaction to intent-centric collaboration. SAP S/4HANA remains the trusted System of Record; HANA-X Copilot becomes the Enterprise Intent Layer and System of Intent; the HANA-X Agentic OS provides governed execution.

## Primary audience

- SAP leadership;
- SAP S/4HANA customers;
- enterprise executives and SAP decision-makers;
- enterprise and solution architects;
- implementation partners and engineering teams.

## Publication objectives

1. Introduce HANA-X Copilot and the Project ICE thesis.
2. Establish HANA-X's vision for AI-powered SAP operations.
3. Demonstrate Clean Core-aligned integration through standard SAP interfaces.
4. Present a full enterprise architecture and endpoint strategy.
5. Use procurement as the flagship business case while acknowledging the broader S/4HANA API landscape.
6. Provide a traceable foundation for product requirements and implementation.

## Core principles

- Executive-first communication.
- Technical claims must be defensible and implementable.
- Clean Core by design.
- Human-in-the-loop governance for consequential actions.
- Model choice and deployment flexibility, including on-premises operation.
- Security, explainability, auditability, and least privilege.
- Reusable architecture across SAP business domains and future enterprise systems.

## Primary deliverables

- Master Authoring Guide;
- detailed table of contents and chapter briefs;
- 60–80 page flagship technical publication;
- technical appendices and SAP endpoint catalog;
- requirements traceability matrix;
- architecture and figure catalog;
- executive presentation and supporting product artifacts.

## Success criteria

The publication must be suitable for SAP leadership, credible to enterprise architects, grounded in verifiable sources, visually coherent, internally consistent, and directly traceable to product requirements and architectural components.

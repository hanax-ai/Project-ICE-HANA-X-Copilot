# Executive Summary Authoring Standard

**Parent artifact:** ICE-GOV-002  
**Addendum:** ICE-GOV-002-ADD-001  
**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** IN-REVIEW

## 1. Purpose

This addendum defines the authoring standard for `ICE-FM-002 - Executive Summary`. It adapts the concise, decision-oriented principles in `ICE-SRC-012` to the needs of a flagship technical publication for SAP leadership, enterprise executives, architects, customers, and implementation partners.

## 2. Governing principle

The Executive Summary is not an introduction and not a component inventory. It is the publication's complete strategic argument in compressed form. Every sentence must answer at least one of the following questions:

- What urgent enterprise problem exists?
- Why does it matter now?
- What is HANA-X Copilot?
- Why is the solution credible and differentiated?
- What measurable value will the procurement proof point test?
- What strategic action is requested?

Content that does not advance one of these questions should be removed or moved to another chapter.

## 3. Two-layer structure

### 3.1 Page 1 - Executive Decision Brief

Target 300-500 words. It must stand alone and be readable in approximately sixty seconds.

Required elements:

1. **Strategic imperative:** the distance between business intent and governed SAP execution.
2. **HANA-X response:** the Enterprise Intent Layer for SAP S/4HANA.
3. **Business value:** velocity, adoption, value realization, Clean Core, control, sovereignty, and extensibility.
4. **Proof point:** procurement as the initial measurable domain.
5. **Ask:** approval to proceed with the governed procurement proof of value and publication program.

### 3.2 Pages 2-3 - Supporting Executive Summary

Target 800-1,200 words. It may provide concise depth on:

- Intent-Centric Enterprise Computing;
- SAP as the System of Record and HANA-X as the System of Intent;
- human-AI co-working and intent clarification;
- governed execution, policy, authorization, approval, and audit;
- Clean Core-aligned SAP integration;
- customer-controlled model deployment;
- procurement outcomes and measurement; and
- cross-domain expansion.

## 4. Scannability standard

- Use meaningful section headings.
- Use bold anchor phrases at the start of compact bullets.
- Use one callout for the principal takeaway.
- Prefer short paragraphs and concrete language.
- The first page must communicate the problem, solution, value, proof point, and ask during a five-second visual scan.
- Avoid a wall of text, decorative jargon, and long technical lists.

## 5. Evidence and metrics

The Executive Summary may use:

- verified facts with approved citations;
- pilot targets clearly labeled as targets; and
- expected benefits expressed as design intent or testable hypotheses.

It must not invent ROI, cost reduction, cycle-time improvement, revenue protection, or adoption figures. Point-in-time market capitalization, valuation multiples, and share prices are excluded from the evergreen first-page narrative.

Examples:

- Acceptable before pilot evidence: "The procurement pilot will measure task duration, invalid submissions, manual handoffs, approval compliance, exception recovery, and user trust."
- Not acceptable without evidence: "HANA-X Copilot will reduce procurement costs by 40 percent."

## 6. Technical-content boundary

The first page may state the operating model in one sentence but must not enumerate frameworks, databases, protocols, model families, or infrastructure components.

Approved credibility pattern:

> HANA-X Copilot operates as a governed Enterprise Intent Layer above SAP S/4HANA, using released SAP interfaces, policy-controlled tools, human approval, and customer-controlled AI infrastructure while preserving SAP as the System of Record.

Detailed component descriptions belong in Part III and Part IV.

## 7. Required call to action

The closing section must state a concrete decision. For the first publication draft, the recommended ask is:

> Approve the HANA-X Copilot reference architecture and publication direction as the foundation for a governed procurement proof of value, including target-system endpoint validation, security review, and pilot implementation planning.

The ask may be revised when a customer-specific budget, sponsor, pilot window, or implementation commitment exists.

## 8. Prohibited patterns

- Beginning with company history or generic AI trends.
- Treating the Executive Summary as a chapter-by-chapter synopsis.
- Withholding the conclusion until the final paragraph.
- Listing technology components on page 1.
- Using unsupported financial projections.
- Claiming guaranteed success, savings, or productivity.
- Using "zero technical debt" to mean the absence of technical detail.
- Describing HANA-X Copilot as a chatbot.

## 9. Acceptance test

An executive who reads only page 1 must be able to repeat:

1. the SAP customer problem;
2. the HANA-X Copilot solution;
3. the principal business value;
4. the procurement proof point; and
5. the requested next action.

The complete Executive Summary must be readable in five minutes or less and remain consistent with the Founder Letter, business-driver artifact, approved terminology, architecture, SAP endpoint strategy, and Claims Register.

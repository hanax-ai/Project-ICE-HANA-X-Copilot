# ICE-GOV-003 — Editorial Style Guide

**Status:** DRAFT SCAFFOLD  
**Version:** 0.1

## Initial standards

- Write for executives first, with sufficient depth for architects and implementation teams.
- Use authoritative, practical, technically rigorous language without unsupported hype.
- Introduce business context before implementation detail.
- Use `SAP S/4HANA`, never `S/4 Hana` in publication text.
- Use `on-premises` for deployment; do not use `on-premise` as an adjective.
- Describe Ollama as a local model-serving and management runtime, not as a model family.
- Distinguish established SAP capabilities, HANA-X architecture decisions, and forward-looking concepts.
- Avoid absolute competitive or cost claims until the Claims Register records approved evidence and wording.
- Preserve human agency: consequential transactions remain subject to policy and approval controls.
- Provide alt text for every figure and accessible headings and tables.

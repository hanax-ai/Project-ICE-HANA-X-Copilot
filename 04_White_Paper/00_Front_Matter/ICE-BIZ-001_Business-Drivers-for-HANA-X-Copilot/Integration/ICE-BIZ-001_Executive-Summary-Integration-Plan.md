# ICE-BIZ-001 - Executive Summary Integration Plan

**Target:** `ICE-FM-002 - Executive Summary`  
**Status:** Proposed input

## Recommended role

The Executive Summary should use the business-driver artifact to answer three questions before describing the architecture:

1. Why must enterprise interaction change now?
2. What problem remains unresolved for SAP customers?
3. Why is HANA-X Copilot a credible and differentiated response?

## Recommended placement

Place a concise business-driver passage immediately after the opening definition of Intent-Centric Enterprise Computing and before the technical architecture summary. The passage should be approximately 250-350 words.

## Recommended Executive Summary language

SAP S/4HANA remains the operational system of record for some of the world's most consequential business processes. Yet users still bear much of the burden of translating a business outcome into applications, transactions, fields, approvals, and exception paths. At the same time, enterprise software competition is shifting toward agentic AI: systems that do more than answer questions and can coordinate governed work.

HANA-X Copilot addresses that gap through an Enterprise Intent Layer between the user and SAP S/4HANA. A user states the desired outcome in natural language. The Copilot resolves entities, asks targeted clarification questions, retrieves authorized context, presents an explainable execution plan, applies policy and approval controls, and invokes registered tools through released SAP interfaces. SAP remains the System of Record; HANA-X becomes the System of Intent.

The business case is grounded in value realization. HANA-X Copilot is designed to reduce interaction friction, accelerate process completion, improve use of standard SAP capabilities, preserve Clean Core, and offer a customer-controlled on-premises AI deployment model. Procurement provides the first measurable proof point, with the same governed architecture extending to finance, sales, manufacturing, supply chain, HR, and executive decision support.

## Claims discipline

The Executive Summary should not include point-in-time market capitalization, P/E ratios, dividend yields, or unqualified statements that HANA-X will reverse SAP backlog trends. It may state that cloud-backlog momentum and agentic-AI positioning are important market signals, subject to citations and claims review.

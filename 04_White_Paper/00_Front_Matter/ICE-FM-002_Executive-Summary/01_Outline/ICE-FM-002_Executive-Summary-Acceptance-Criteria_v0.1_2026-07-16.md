# ICE-FM-002 - Executive Summary Acceptance Criteria

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** DRAFT  
**Date:** 16 July 2026

The Executive Summary cannot advance to `IN-REVIEW` unless all intake criteria are satisfied.

## Strategic completeness

- [ ] The first page states the urgent enterprise problem.
- [ ] HANA-X Copilot is defined in one clear sentence.
- [ ] The Enterprise Intent Layer is explained in plain language.
- [ ] SAP remains clearly identified as the System of Record.
- [ ] HANA-X is clearly identified as the System of Intent.
- [ ] HANA-X Copilot is distinguished from a chatbot.
- [ ] Procurement is presented as the initial measurable proof point.
- [ ] The closing section states a specific strategic ask.

## Executive usability

- [ ] Page 1 can be understood without reading the rest of the publication.
- [ ] Page 1 is 300-500 words and readable in approximately sixty seconds.
- [ ] The complete summary is readable in five minutes or less.
- [ ] Headings and bold anchor phrases support a five-second visual scan.
- [ ] The page does not present a dense wall of text.
- [ ] No component inventory or technical deep dive appears on page 1.

## Evidence and claims

- [ ] Every quantitative claim has a governed source and claim record.
- [ ] Pilot measures are labeled as targets or evaluation criteria, not achieved results.
- [ ] Expected benefits use qualified wording until measured.
- [ ] No unsupported ROI, savings, productivity, adoption, or revenue number appears.
- [ ] No point-in-time valuation metric appears in the evergreen first-page narrative.
- [ ] No unsupported high-risk competitive claim appears.

## Architecture and SAP alignment

- [ ] The summary is consistent with ICE-ARCH-001 and ICE-ARCH-002.
- [ ] Clean Core is described as an architecture discipline rather than a universal guarantee.
- [ ] SAP execution is described as occurring through released and governed interfaces.
- [ ] Human approval, policy, authorization, and audit are represented accurately.
- [ ] Customer-controlled deployment is expressed as an option, not as cost-free infrastructure.

## Governance and production

- [ ] All source identities and artifact relationships are correct.
- [ ] Editorial, architecture, SAP, security, source, claims, executive, visual, and accessibility intake reviews are assigned.
- [ ] The Markdown draft renders cleanly into the publication format.
- [ ] All callouts, headings, and lists meet accessibility requirements.
- [ ] No unresolved BLOCKER comment remains.

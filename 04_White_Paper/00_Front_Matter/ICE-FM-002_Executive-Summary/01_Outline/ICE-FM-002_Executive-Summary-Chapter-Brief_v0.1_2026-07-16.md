## Objective

Make the complete strategic case for HANA-X Copilot in a form that a time-constrained SAP or enterprise executive can understand, repeat, and act upon without reading the remaining publication.

## Primary reader

SAP leadership, SAP customers, enterprise executives, chief information and AI officers, and strategic implementation partners.

## Reader outcome

The reader should understand:

- why the gap between business intent and SAP execution matters;
- what Intent-Centric Enterprise Computing means;
- how HANA-X Copilot differs from a chatbot;
- why the architecture strengthens rather than replaces SAP;
- what procurement will prove; and
- what decision or next action is requested.

## Format and budget

- Page 1: Executive Decision Brief, 300-500 words.
- Pages 2-3: Supporting Executive Summary, 800-1,200 words.
- Total target: 1,100-1,700 words, subject to rendered page review.
- Reading time: page 1 in approximately sixty seconds; complete summary in five minutes or less.

## Required messages

1. SAP S/4HANA remains the trusted System of Record.
2. The unresolved problem is the friction between business intent and governed execution.
3. HANA-X Copilot provides the Enterprise Intent Layer and System of Intent.
4. The product interprets, clarifies, plans, applies policy, obtains approval, and executes through registered SAP tools.
5. The business value is velocity with control, not autonomy without governance.
6. Procurement is the initial measurable proof point.
7. The platform expands through reusable controls and domain-specific tools.
8. The immediate ask is approval to proceed with the governed proof-of-value and publication program.

## Required proof model

The summary may identify the following pilot measures without claiming results:

- median task duration;
- clarification turns;
- invalid or incomplete submission rate;
- manual handoffs;
- approval compliance;
- exception recovery time;
- audit completeness; and
- user trust and repeat use.

## Exclusions

- Detailed framework and component inventory on page 1.
- Unsupported financial projections or ROI.
- Installation, infrastructure, schema, or endpoint detail.
- New competitive-superlative categories.
- Unqualified promises of guaranteed savings, deterministic AI, or universal SAP support.

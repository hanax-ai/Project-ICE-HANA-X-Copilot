## Proposed sequence

1. **The enterprise interaction problem** - business users still translate outcomes into transactions, applications, fields, and approvals.
2. **Intent-Centric Enterprise Computing** - define ICE and the transition from transaction-centric and process-centric interaction.
3. **The business urgency** - cloud value realization, agentic-AI competition, user adoption, and the need to protect existing SAP investment.
4. **The HANA-X response** - Enterprise Intent Layer, co-working clarification, governed execution, and SAP as System of Record.
5. **Why the architecture is credible** - on-premises model runtime, Clean Core interfaces, policy, approval, audit, and multi-MCP tools.
6. **The proof point** - procurement as the first measurable domain.
7. **The expansion thesis** - reusable platform for finance, sales, manufacturing, supply chain, HR, and executive agents.
8. **The publication promise** - architecture, requirements, controls, implementation guidance, and evidence traceability.
